CHIEF COMPLAINT
Chest tightness and palpitations, episodic — 2 months.

HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS
Patricia is a 58-year-old female with hypertension and hypothyroidism who reports a
2-month history of episodic chest tightness and palpitations. Episodes occur 3–4 times
per week, last 2–10 minutes, and resolve spontaneously. She denies syncope or presyncope.
She notes associated diaphoresis and mild dyspnea during episodes. The episodes seem
unrelated to exertion — occurring at rest as well. She denies jaw pain or arm pain. Her
levothyroxine was increased 3 months ago by her prior PCP.

MEDICATIONS
- Levothyroxine 125 mcg daily (recently increased from 100 mcg)
- Lisinopril 10 mg daily

ALLERGIES: NKDA

DIAGNOSTIC RESULTS

ASSESSMENT
1. Iatrogenic hyperthyroidism — over-replacement with levothyroxine (suppressed TSH,
   elevated FT4/FT3); precipitating palpitations, tachycardia, lid lag, tremor
2. Hypertension — suboptimally controlled

PLAN
1. Reduce levothyroxine to 100 mcg daily (prior dose). Counsel patient not to stop abruptly.
2. Recheck TSH/FT4 in 6 weeks. Target TSH 1.0–2.5 for Hashimoto's patient.
3. Atenolol 25 mg daily — short-term beta-blocker for tachycardia/palpitations until
   thyroid levels normalize. Wean off once euthyroid.
4. Cardiology referral deferred — likely to resolve with thyroid optimization. If EKG
   changes occur or symptoms persist, will refer.
5. Holter monitor ×24 hours — to characterize palpitation episodes while symptomatic.
6. Increase lisinopril to 20 mg daily for BP.
7. Follow-up: 6 weeks with labs.

Patient counseled on importance of not adjusting levothyroxine dose independently.
