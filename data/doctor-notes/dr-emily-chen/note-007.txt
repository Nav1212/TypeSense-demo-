CHIEF COMPLAINT
Recurrent headaches, 3 months.

HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS
Alice presents with a 3-month history of recurrent headaches occurring 2–3 times per week.
She describes them as unilateral (left-sided), pulsating, moderate-to-severe in intensity
(6–7/10), lasting 4–12 hours when untreated, associated with nausea and photophobia but
no phonophobia. She reports a premonitory visual aura (zigzag lights in peripheral vision)
lasting 15–20 minutes before about half of her headaches. Headaches are exacerbated by
physical activity. She finds relief in a dark, quiet room. OTC ibuprofen provides partial
relief. No neck stiffness, no fever, no focal neurological deficits. She denies positional
component. Sleep disturbance reported.

MEDICATIONS: Ibuprofen 400 mg PRN (using 4–5×/week — concern for medication overuse)
ALLERGIES: NKDA

PHYSICAL EXAMINATION
Vitals: BP 118/72, HR 70, Temp 36.7°C, Wt 62 kg
Neurological: CNII-XII intact; visual fields normal to confrontation; no papilledema on
              fundoscopy; gait normal; no focal deficits; no meningismus

ASSESSMENT
1. Migraine with aura — classic presentation; meets ICHD-3 criteria
2. Possible medication overuse headache (MOH) — ibuprofen use ≥10–15 days/month

PLAN
1. Acute treatment: Sumatriptan 50 mg PO at headache onset (can repeat ×1 after 2 hours
   if insufficient). Limit triptan use to ≤10 days/month. Discontinue ibuprofen overuse
   (abrupt withdrawal may worsen headache for 1–2 weeks — counselled).
2. Preventive therapy: Topiramate 25 mg nightly ×2 weeks, then 25 mg BID. Aim to reduce
   frequency ≥50%. Alternative: propranolol 40 mg BID (discussed both; patient prefers
   topiramate as she wants weight-neutral option).
3. Trigger diary: Identify personal triggers (stress, sleep deprivation, menstrual cycle,
   red wine/caffeine). Sleep hygiene counselled.
4. Referral to neurology if inadequate response to treatment in 3 months.
5. MRI brain with/without contrast ordered — to exclude secondary cause given relatively
   new onset and high frequency; low clinical suspicion but appropriate given history.
6. Follow-up: 6–8 weeks.

Patient counselled on triptan pharmacology and what to expect with preventive medications.
Understood. Appointment confirmed.
