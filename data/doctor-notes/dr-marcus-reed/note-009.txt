Patient: Frank D., 49M

CHIEF COMPLAINT
Snoring, excessive daytime sleepiness — wife concerned.

HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS
Frank is a 49-year-old male with obesity and hypertension who presents after his wife
insists he be evaluated for possible sleep apnea. She reports loud snoring every night and
has witnessed apneic episodes (him "stopping breathing" for up to 30 seconds). Frank
reports he falls asleep within 5 minutes of sitting down, has fallen asleep at his desk at
work, and nearly fell asleep driving last week. He reports unrefreshing sleep despite 7–8
hours in bed. He denies cataplexy or sleep paralysis. He has mild morning headaches.
Epworth Sleepiness Scale completed today: 18/24 (severe daytime sleepiness).

PAST MEDICAL HISTORY: Hypertension (on amlodipine); Obesity (BMI 38)
MEDICATIONS: Amlodipine 10 mg daily; ALLERGIES: NKDA

PHYSICAL EXAMINATION
Vitals: BP 148/92 (high — worse control than usual), HR 84, Neck circumference: 44 cm (↑)
Wt 112 kg, BMI 38.2
Oropharynx: Mallampati class 3; crowded oropharynx; elongated soft palate; enlarged uvula
             (mild); tonsillar hypertrophy grade 1; macroglossia mild
Nasal: Mild septal deviation right; nasal turbinate mild enlargement

ASSESSMENT
1. Suspected obstructive sleep apnea (OSA) — highly likely given: witnessed apneas,
   ESS 18, large neck circumference, crowded oropharynx, obesity, hypertension
2. Hypertension — suboptimally controlled; OSA is a major contributor
3. Obesity BMI 38 — driving OSA and hypertension

PLAN
1. Home sleep test (HST) ordered — AutoPAP level III device; patient instructed on use.
   If HST inconclusive, refer for full in-lab PSG.
2. Increase amlodipine to 10 mg daily (already at maximum); add losartan 50 mg daily
   for BP control (dual therapy; also beneficial in OSA patients).
3. Sleep hygiene: Lateral sleeping position; elevate head of bed 30°; avoid alcohol
   and sedatives within 3 hours of sleep.
4. Weight loss counselling — even 10% reduction can significantly improve AHI.
   Refer to structured weight management program.
5. ADVISE: Avoid driving or operating machinery until sleep apnea is assessed and treated —
   documented in chart.
6. Follow-up: 2–3 weeks with HST results. If AHI >5, CPAP/APAP therapy to be initiated.
7. If CPAP initiated: ENT referral if CPAP intolerant (surgical options: UPPP, Inspire implant).
