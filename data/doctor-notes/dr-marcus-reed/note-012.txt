Skin:
  - Trunk (chest, abdomen, back): Multiple salmon-pink plaques 1–3 cm with collarette

  - Arms and thighs: Smaller similar lesions following skin tension lines (Langer's lines)
  - Notable larger oval plaque (4×5 cm) on left flank — preceded smaller lesions ("herald patch")
  - Face, palms, soles: Spared
  - No vesicles; no pustules; no petechiae; no mucosal involvement
Lymph nodes: Mild bilateral axillary lymphadenopathy (soft, mobile, non-tender)
Oropharynx: No oral ulcers; no exudate

ASSESSMENT
1. Pityriasis rosea — classic presentation: herald patch, salmon-pink collarette plaques
   in Christmas tree pattern following Langer's lines, post-URI onset (associated with
   HHV-6/7 reactivation), sparing of face, palms, soles
2. Must rule out secondary syphilis (can mimic pityriasis rosea, involves palms/soles;
   syphilis in this case: palms/soles spared → less likely but screening warranted)

PLAN
1. Reassure: Pityriasis rosea is self-limiting — resolves in 6–12 weeks with no treatment.
2. RPR/VDRL sent (to exclude secondary syphilis — standard of care even with classic PR).
3. Symptomatic treatment: Cetirizine 10 mg daily (continue); menthol 0.5% cream PRN
   for itch; cool oatmeal baths for itch relief.
4. UV phototherapy (narrowband UVB) — if severe or persistent beyond 8 weeks, refer
   dermatology. Not indicated at this time.
5. Avoid hot showers/baths (can worsen pruritus).
6. Reassure: Not contagious person-to-person; no isolation needed.
7. Follow-up: 4 weeks or sooner if rash worsens, fever develops, or RPR returns positive.
8. If syphilis serology positive — refer immediately to GUM/STI clinic for treatment.

Patient reassured. RPR results to be communicated by phone.
